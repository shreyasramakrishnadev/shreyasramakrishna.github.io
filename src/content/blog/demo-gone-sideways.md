Replace this with a real story. The strongest version of this post follows
a simple shape:

## What happened

Set the scene in a sentence or two. What was the demo, who was in the room,
what went wrong.

## What I did in the moment

The specific, small decision you made — not the polished version, the real
one. This is the part readers actually remember.

## What I'd change next time

One or two concrete lessons. Skip the generic "communication is key" — get
specific to your situation.

> Tip: posts like this work because they're honest about the mess, not
> because they end in a clean win.
